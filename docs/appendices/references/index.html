References and Resources

This appendix provides a curated list of references, documentation, and resources that support the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics curriculum.

Official Documentation​

ROS 2 Documentation​

  • ROS 2 Documentation: https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/

    • Comprehensive guides, tutorials, and API documentation for ROS 2 Humble Hawksbill
    • Essential for understanding core concepts, packages, and best practices

  • ROS 2 Tutorials: https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/Tutorials.html

    • Step-by-step tutorials for beginners and advanced users
    • Covers topics from basic concepts to advanced system integration

  • URDF Documentation: https://wiki.ros.org/urdf

    • Unified Robot Description Format specifications and tutorials
    • Critical for robot modeling and simulation

Gazebo Documentation​

  • Gazebo Harmonic Documentation: https://gazebosim.org/docs/harmonic

    • Latest documentation for Gazebo Harmonic simulation environment
    • Includes tutorials, API documentation, and best practices

  • Gazebo Tutorials: https://gazebosim.org/tutorials

    • Comprehensive tutorials for simulation setup and usage
    • Covers sensor simulation, physics, and robot integration

NVIDIA Isaac Documentation​

  • Isaac Sim Documentation: https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/isaacsim/latest/index.html

    • Complete guide to NVIDIA Isaac Sim on Omniverse
    • Includes installation, usage, and scripting guides

  • Isaac ROS Documentation: https://nvidia-isaac-ros.github.io/repositories_and_packages.html

    • Hardware-accelerated ROS packages for perception and navigation
    • Essential for leveraging NVIDIA GPU acceleration in robotics

  • Isaac ROS Gardens: https://nvidia-isaac-ros.github.io/concepts/index.html

    • Conceptual guides and best practices for Isaac ROS packages
    • Explains the underlying technology and implementation details

Docusaurus Documentation​

  • Docusaurus v3 Documentation: https://docusaurus.io/docs/v3
    • Complete guide to building static sites with Docusaurus
    • Includes customization, deployment, and theming guides

Academic Papers and Research​

Foundational Papers​

  • "ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System" - Morgan Quigley, et al. (2009)

    • The original paper describing the Robot Operating System concept
    • Available: http://www.cs.stanford.edu/group/manips/ICRA2009_Quigley.pdf

  • "The Open Motion Planning Library" - Ioan Șucan, et al. (2012)

    • Foundational paper on motion planning algorithms used in MoveIt and navigation
    • Available: https://ompl.kavrakilab.org/

  • "A Survey of Robot Learning from Demonstration" - Brenna Argall, et al. (2009)

    • Comprehensive survey on learning from demonstration techniques
    • Critical for understanding humanoid robot learning approaches

Vision-Language-Action Research​

  • "Vision-Language Models in Robotics: A Survey" - Mohamed El Banani, et al. (2024)

    • Recent survey on VLA models in robotics applications
    • Available: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.11838

  • "PaLM-E: An Embodied Multimodal Language Model" - Danny Driess, et al. (2023)

    • Paper on embodied language models for robotics
    • Available: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.03378

  • "RT-2: Vision-Language-Action Models for Real-World Robot Manipulation" - Michael Ahn, et al. (2023)

    • Research on VLA models for real-world robot manipulation
    • Available: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.15818

Humanoid Robotics Research​

  • "Humanoid Robotics: A Reference" - Ambarish Goswami and Prahlad Vadakkepat (2016)

    • Comprehensive reference on humanoid robotics concepts
    • Covers locomotion, balance, and control

  • "The Development of Humanoid Robotics in Japan and the U.S." - Hiroyuki Ishii (2005)

    • Historical perspective on humanoid robotics development
    • Available: https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/jsmeij/12/3/12_3_2/_article

  • "Balance Control and Whole-Body Motion Control for Humanoid Robots" - Shuuji Kajita, et al. (2006)

    • Key paper on balance and motion control for humanoid robots
    • Available: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1641789

Simulation and Digital Twin Research​

  • "Simulation Approaches in Robotics" - Keng Peng Tee, et al. (2008)

    • Survey of simulation techniques in robotics
    • Available: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/4516982

  • "Digital Twin in Industrial Internet of Things" - Fei Tao, et al. (2017)

    • Foundational paper on digital twin concepts
    • Available: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/7984010

  • "Domain Randomization for Transferring Deep Neural Networks from Simulation to the Real World" - Josh Tobin, et al. (2017)

    • Key paper on domain randomization for sim-to-real transfer
    • Available: https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.06907

Books and Textbooks​

Core Robotics Textbooks​

  • "Probabilistic Robotics" by Sebastian Thrun, Wolfram Burgard, and Dieter Fox

    • Comprehensive coverage of probabilistic techniques in robotics
    • Essential for understanding localization, mapping, and planning

  • "Springer Handbook of Robotics" edited by Bruno Siciliano and Oussama Khatib

    • Comprehensive reference covering all aspects of robotics
    • Updated regularly with latest developments

  • "Robotics, Vision and Control" by Peter Corke

    • Practical approach to robotics with MATLAB and Python examples
    • Good for understanding fundamental concepts with code examples

  • "Introduction to Autonomous Mobile Robots" by Roland Siegwart, Illah Nourbakhsh, and Davide Scaramuzza

    • Focuses on mobile robot algorithms and systems
    • Good foundation for navigation and control concepts

AI and Machine Learning for Robotics​

  • "Robot Learning" by Andrew Barto and Richard Sutton

    • Focuses on reinforcement learning applications in robotics
    • Available: https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/introduction-reinforcement-learning-second-edition

  • "Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications" by Richard Szeliski

    • Comprehensive guide to computer vision techniques
    • Essential for understanding robot perception

  • "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville

    • Comprehensive textbook on deep learning
    • Critical for understanding neural networks in robotics

Humanoid Robotics Specific​

  • "Humanoid Robots: Modeling and Control" by Ambarish Goswami and Mingxing Xin

    • Focuses specifically on humanoid robot modeling and control
    • Covers kinematics, dynamics, and control strategies

  • "Human Walking" by David Winter

    • Biomechanics of human walking, important for humanoid locomotion
    • Provides insights for creating natural-looking humanoid movements

Online Courses and Educational Resources​

MOOCs and Online Courses​

  • ETH Zurich - Introduction to Mobile Robotics

    • Available on Coursera
    • Covers fundamentals of mobile robotics including navigation and perception

  • University of Pennsylvania - Robotics Specialization

    • Available on Coursera
    • Comprehensive robotics education covering kinematics, dynamics, and control

  • Columbia University - Robotics MicroMasters

    • Available on edX
    • Advanced robotics education with practical applications

YouTube Channels and Video Resources​

  • ROBOTIS Open Source

    • Channel with tutorials on ROS, robot programming, and hardware
    • Practical examples and demonstrations

  • The Construct

    • ROS tutorials and simulation examples
    • Hands-on learning with Gazebo and real robots

  • NVIDIA Technical Blog Videos

    • Videos on Isaac Sim, Isaac ROS, and AI for robotics
    • Practical examples and demonstrations

Software Tools and Libraries​

Development Environments​

  • Visual Studio Code: https://code.visualstudio.com/

    • Popular code editor with excellent ROS 2 extensions
    • Supports debugging, version control, and various programming languages

  • PyCharm: https://www.jetbrains.com/pycharm/

    • Python IDE with ROS 2 integration capabilities
    • Good for Python-based robotics development

  • CLion: https://www.jetbrains.com/clion/

    • C++ IDE with ROS 2 workspace integration
    • Excellent for C++ based ROS 2 packages

Version Control and Collaboration​

  • Git: https://git-scm.com/

    • Distributed version control system
    • Essential for collaborative robotics development

  • GitHub: https://github.com/

    • Platform for hosting and collaborating on code
    • Many robotics projects and tutorials available

  • GitLab: https://about.gitlab.com/

    • Alternative to GitHub with CI/CD capabilities
    • Good for private robotics projects

Simulation Tools​

  • Gazebo: https://gazebosim.org/

    • Open-source robotics simulator with physics engine
    • Standard tool for ROS-based simulation

  • Isaac Sim: https://developer.nvidia.com/isaac-sim

    • NVIDIA's high-fidelity simulation environment
    • Built on Omniverse platform with photorealistic rendering

  • Unity Robotics Hub: https://github.com/Unity-Technologies/Unity-Robotics-Hub

    • Integration between Unity and ROS 2
    • High-fidelity visualization and simulation

AI and Machine Learning Libraries​

  • TensorFlow: https://www.tensorflow.org/

    • Open-source machine learning framework
    • Used for training perception and control models

  • PyTorch: https://pytorch.org/

    • Deep learning framework with Python interface
    • Popular for research and prototyping

  • OpenCV: https://opencv.org/

    • Computer vision library with robotics applications
    • Essential for image processing and computer vision

  • scikit-learn: https://scikit-learn.org/

    • Machine learning library for Python
    • Good for traditional ML approaches in robotics

Hardware Platforms and Resources​

Open-Source Humanoid Platforms​

  • Poppy Project: https://poppy-project.org/

    • Open-source 3D printed humanoid robots
    • Good for research and education

  • NAO Robot: https://www.ald.softbankrobotics.com/en/robots/nao

    • Commercial humanoid robot platform
    • Extensive documentation and community support

  • Pepper Robot: https://www.ald.softbankrobotics.com/en/robots/pepper

    • Humanoid robot for human interaction
    • Good for studying HRI applications

ROS-Compatible Platforms​

  • TurtleBot Series: https://turtlebot.github.io/

    • Entry-level mobile robots for ROS learning
    • Extensive tutorials and community support

  • UR Series Robots: https://www.universal-robots.com/

    • Collaborative robots with ROS support
    • Good for manipulation learning

  • Fetch Robotics: http://fetchrobotics.com/

    • Mobile manipulation platform with ROS support
    • Good for advanced manipulation research

Community Resources​

Forums and Discussion Platforms​

  • ROS Answers: https://answers.ros.org/questions/

    • Official Q&A forum for ROS users
    • Great place to get help with ROS-specific issues

  • Robotics Stack Exchange: https://robotics.stackexchange.com/

    • Q&A site for robotics professionals and researchers
    • Covers theoretical and practical robotics topics

  • Isaac ROS Forum: https://forums.developer.nvidia.com/c/agx-autonomous-machines/isaac/25

    • NVIDIA's forum for Isaac ROS users
    • Good for hardware-accelerated robotics questions

Conferences and Journals​

  • IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA)

    • Premier conference for robotics research
    • Annual publication of latest advances

  • IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS)

    • Major robotics conference with focus on intelligent systems
    • Good venue for humanoid robotics research

  • International Journal of Robotics Research (IJRR)

    • Leading journal in robotics research
    • Publishes high-quality theoretical and applied research

  • IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters (RA-L)

    • Rapid publication venue for robotics research
    • Good for timely robotics advances

Additional Resources​

Online Tutorials and Guides​

  • The Construct ROS Tutorials: https://www.theconstructsim.com/

    • Interactive ROS tutorials with simulation environments
    • Good for hands-on learning

  • ROS 2 Beginner Tutorials: https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/Tutorials/Beginner-CLI-Tools.html

    • Official ROS 2 tutorials for beginners
    • Essential for getting started with ROS 2

  • MoveIt Tutorials: https://moveit.picknik.ai/humble/doc/

    • Tutorials for motion planning and manipulation
    • Essential for manipulation tasks

Sample Code and Repositories​

  • ROS 2 Examples: https://github.com/ros2/examples

    • Official examples for ROS 2 concepts
    • Good starting point for learning ROS 2 patterns

  • Isaac ROS Examples: https://github.com/NVIDIA-ISAAC-ROS

    • NVIDIA's Isaac ROS package examples
    • Essential for hardware-accelerated robotics

  • Universal Robot ROS 2: https://github.com/UniversalRobots/Universal_Robots_ROS2_Driver

    • ROS 2 driver examples for industrial robots
    • Good reference for real hardware integration

Standards and Best Practices​

ROS 2 Conventions​

  • REP 2003: https://www.ros.org/reps/rep-2003.html

    • ROS 2 system architecture and design principles
    • Essential for understanding ROS 2 philosophy

  • REP 144: https://www.ros.org/reps/rep-144.html

    • Standard Coordinate Systems for Robotics
    • Critical for proper frame conventions

  • ROS 2 Style Guide: https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/The-ROS2-Project/Contributing/Code-Style-Language-Versions.html

    • Coding standards for ROS 2 packages
    • Important for collaborative development

Safety and Ethics​

  • ISO 13482: https://www.iso.org/standard/45286.html

    • Safety requirements for personal care robots
    • Important for humanoid robot deployment

  • IEEE Standards for Ethical AI: https://ethicsinaction.ieee.org/

    • Guidelines for ethical AI and robotics development
    • Important for responsible robotics

Quality Assurance​

  • Robotics Testing Best Practices: Various publications from ROS-I consortium

    • Guidelines for testing and validation of robotic systems
    • Critical for reliable robot deployment

  • Simulation Validation Techniques: Literature on sim-to-real transfer

    • Methods for validating simulation results
    • Important for safe robotics development

This list will continue to grow as new resources become relevant to the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics curriculum. Students are encouraged to contribute additional resources they find valuable to this living document.
